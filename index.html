What is Materials Handling?
▾

Materials handling and storage is a technique that includes lifting, placing, storing, or movement of materials through physical strength or appropriate handling equipment.

It has two general classifications:

  • Manual Materials Handling (MMH) — moving things by lifting, lowering, pushing, pulling, carrying, holding, or restraining using physical strength
  • Mechanical Materials Handling — using rigid, manually or mechanically-powered equipment mainly for bulky and heavy items
⚠️ MMH is the most common cause of occupational fatigue, low back pain, and lower back injuries. It can lead to strains, sprains, wounds, fractures, and hernias.
2
What Makes MMH Hazardous?
▾

The hazard level depends on what you're handling, what the task is, and what conditions are at the workplace.

Hazardous Load Characteristics

  • Too heavy, too high or too low for a safe lift
  • Too big, wet, slippery, or has sharp edges
  • Unstable — center of gravity can shift (e.g., a partially filled drum)

Hazardous Task Factors

  • Poor lifting technique — back bent, twisting, reaching too far
  • Moving material over long distances without rest
  • Combination of tasks: lifting + carrying + lowering

Hazardous Work Conditions

  • Uneven, wet, slippery, or icy surfaces
  • Differences in floor levels
  • Poor housekeeping, inadequate lighting
  • Cold, hot, humid, or windy environment
  • Fast pace of work or restricted movement
3
Proper Lifting Techniques
▾

Before You Lift

  • Check if mechanical aids (hoists, dollies, wheelbarrows) are available
  • Get help with heavy or awkward loads
  • Assess the weight; make sure the load is "free" to move
  • Clear the path — check for grease, water, litter, debris

General Lifting Tips

  • Stand close to the load and face the direction of movement
  • Use a wide stance for balance
  • Keep arms straight, look forward, keep back straight
  • Tighten abdominal muscles; tuck chin into chest
  • Use leg muscles — NOT your back
  • Lift the load as close to the body as possible
  • Lift smoothly — avoid twisting and side bending
🦵 STRONG     🔙 PROTECT IT
Use your legs to lift. Your back is weak under load — protect it always.

Protective Clothing

DO wear: lightweight, tear-resistant clothing; safety boots with toe caps; protective gloves

DO NOT wear: aprons with loose flaps, heavy-duty mitts, clothing with exposed zippers

4
Mechanical Handling Equipment
▾

Mechanical equipment reduces physical effort and makes materials handling easier and safer. Two types: manually-powered and mechanically-powered.

Manually Powered Equipment

  • Hand truck — move bulky objects
  • Platform truck — heavy, irregularly shaped objects
  • Shelf truck — move a variety of objects
  • Semi-live skid — temporary storage
  • Stair climbing truck — moving loads on stairs
  • Pump truck — move palletized materials
  • Dump truck — move and dump waste materials
  • Drum dollies — move and empty drums
  • "A" frame hand truck — handle sheet materials

Mechanically Powered Equipment

  • Lifting equipment: hoists, cranes
  • Transport equipment: forklifts, trailers, conveyors
🔑 Rule: Never operate equipment without proper training. Operators must be certified and authorized.
5
Materials Storage Rules
▾

Storage Room Requirements

  • At least two exits
  • Properly illuminated and ventilated
  • Restricted access

General Storage Rules

  • Store materials neatly and orderly in limited amounts
  • Frequently used materials placed closer to you
  • Store heavy and frequently used items at waist height
  • Do NOT store at floor level
  • Never obstruct: alarm boxes, sprinkler controls, first aid equipment, fuse boxes, aisles, exits

6 Questions Before Moving Material

  • What will be moved?
  • Where to move it?
  • How often will it be moved?
  • How many will be moved?
  • How far will it be moved?
  • How will it be moved (manual or mechanical)?
📌 Key reminder: Obstructed passageways can cause disaster during emergency evacuations. Always keep aisles and exits clear!
